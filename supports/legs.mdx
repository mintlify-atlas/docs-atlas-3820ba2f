---
title: "Leg Supports"
description: "Configure structural leg supports for vertical, horizontal, and spherical pressure vessels. Choose between standard (unbraced) and braced leg configurations with a range of structural profiles."
---

Leg supports consist of individual structural members welded or bolted to the vessel shell or bottom head, extending to a foundation. They are widely used for small to medium vertical vessels, spherical storage tanks, and horizontal vessels where saddles are impractical. PSI supports two leg configurations: **standard (unbraced)** legs and **braced (arriostrada)** legs.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Unbraced legs" icon="table-columns">
    Simple structural sections (rectangular plate, angle, or tube) welded to the vessel. Suitable for short vessels or locations with low lateral loads.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Braced legs" icon="network-wired">
    Structural sections interconnected with horizontal or diagonal bracing. Required when leg slenderness or lateral loading demands additional stiffness.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Parameters

### Leg Configuration

<ParamField path="legQuantity" type="select" required>
  Number of legs supporting the vessel. Options: **3**, **4**, **6**, or **8**.
</ParamField>

<Warning>
  For **horizontal vessels**, `legQuantity` is automatically locked to **4** and cannot be changed. The four legs are arranged in two pairs, with spacing defined by `legLongSpacing` and `legTransSpacing`.
</Warning>

<ParamField path="legProfile" type="select" required>
  Cross-section shape of the structural leg member. Available options depend on the leg configuration:

  - **Unbraced legs:** `Rectangular`, `Angle`, `Tube`
  - **Braced legs:** `Angle (L)`, `Beam (W/I)`, `Pipe (Sch 40/80)`, `Square Tube (HSS)`
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="braceProfile" type="string">
  Designation of the brace member cross-section, using standard structural notation (e.g., `L2x2x1/4` for a 2×2×¼ angle). Only shown for **braced leg** configurations. The brace profile is used to calculate the brace axial capacity and connection forces.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="legLength" type="number" required>
  Unsupported length of each leg from the vessel shell attachment to the base plate. For braced legs, this is the total leg height; bracing tier positions are determined by `bracingTier`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="bracingTier" type="select">
  Number of horizontal bracing levels distributed along the leg height. Options: **1**, **2**, **3**, or **4**. More tiers reduce the effective buckling length of each leg segment. Only shown for **braced leg** configurations.
</ParamField>

### Base Plate (Vertical and Spherical Vessels)

<Info>
  Base plate parameters are shown only for **vertical (columna vertical)** and **spherical (Esférico)** vessel types. Horizontal vessel legs use longitudinal and transverse spacing instead.
</Info>

<ParamField path="legBasePlateWidth" type="number">
  Width of the base plate at the bottom of each leg. The base plate must be sized to limit bearing pressure on the foundation grout.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="legBasePlateLength" type="number">
  Length of the base plate at the bottom of each leg. Together with `legBasePlateWidth`, defines the bearing area and bolt hole layout.
</ParamField>

### Spacing (Horizontal Vessels)

<Info>
  Spacing parameters are only shown when the vessel type is **Horizontal**.
</Info>

<ParamField path="legLongSpacing" type="number">
  Centre-to-centre distance between the two leg pairs measured along the vessel axis (longitudinal direction). This dimension governs the bending moment in the vessel shell between supports.
</ParamField>

<ParamField path="legTransSpacing" type="number">
  Centre-to-centre distance between the two legs within each pair, measured perpendicular to the vessel axis (transverse direction). This dimension affects the stability of each pair under lateral loads.
</ParamField>

### Bolt Circle (Non-Horizontal Vessels)

<ParamField path="legBoltCircle" type="number">
  Diameter of the bolt circle (Dbc) on which the anchor bolts at the leg base plates are positioned. Shown for all vessel types except horizontal.
</ParamField>

<Warning>
  For **spherical vessels**, the bolt circle should be **larger than the sphere outside diameter** to ensure stable geometry. If `legBoltCircle` is entered smaller than the sphere OD and `underSlung` is not enabled, PSI displays a warning: *"Bolt circle (Dbc) is smaller than the sphere diameter. For spherical vessels the bolt circle is typically larger; under-slung legs are unstable and not recommended."*
</Warning>

### Under-Slung Configuration (Spherical Vessels)

<ParamField path="underSlung" type="select">
  Indicates whether the legs are attached below the sphere equator in an under-slung arrangement. Options: **Yes** or **No**. When enabled, the bolt circle warning for small Dbc values is suppressed, as the geometry is intentionally inverted.
</ParamField>

<Note>
  Under-slung leg arrangements are geometrically possible but are generally considered unstable for free-standing spherical vessels. Consult your structural engineer before selecting this option.
</Note>
